Overview

Initial idea. What and Why?

The number of video game consumers increases every year and its market offer is wide, oscillating between consoles (Xbox, PC, switch, PlayStation ...), genres or personal interests. When the user wants to find a video game suitable for the him/her, this issue can sometimes be overwhelming, so an online recommender that shows options according to what the user is looking for is a widely satisfactory tool.

Brainstorming

In this phase I will describe all the possible ideas that may be involved in this project to create an online tool focused on videogames and that helps users find a game that suits their tastes, that is to say, a video game recommender.

  • Classification by genre, favorite video games, console, age, price...
  • Initial test to know what the user likes.
  • Own account:
    • Wish List
    • Profile scoring Systm
  • Possibility to comment and rate the games.
  • Option to purchase within the web or price comparator within the main platforms.
  • Notice when a game goes down in price or a new game suits your tastes.

Exploration Phase

Market Research

In order to get to know what were the inconveniences and advantages to create a project like this, I did my own search basing on very different factors that could imply an impact. Below you can see some example of what I found.

PESTLE

Policy

Video games may have different restrictions depending on the policies of the country.

Commercial regularization such as NAFTA.

Economy

The income and the general purchasing level can be very variable between some countries.

The video game industry is very seasonal and varies greatly throughout the year, with very high and low sales peaks.

Society

People who think they are negative and harmful to the health of those who consume them.

Educational games which are increasing significatively.

Big increase in online video games.

Technology

People are increasingly dependent on technology and have easy access to it.

Legal

Greater control over product regularization.

Greater regularization on electronic waste.

Envirioment

Increased demand for environmentally friendly products. This mainly increases the purchase of digital games and undone the physical ones.

SWOT

Strengths:

Large database with large number of video games regardless of gender or platform.

Weaknesses:

People are not always looking for a new game.
SEO positioning very poor.
Poor interface on most websites

Opportunities:

No connection between the games information and where to buy.
Not clear profile for each type of gamer.
The games have a unique general note. Rating based on narrative, gameplay, visual style… .

Threats:

Piracy.
Similar big platforms.
Not being up to the latest more relevant news.

Surveys and interviews

Not only studying the market is necessary. Knowing the target audience and knowing what their real needs are is essential for the good development of the product. They are the end users, the consumers, they must be heard. I consider that this is the best way to know exactly what they are looking for. For this I conducted personal interviews with a group of regular players and certain online surveys.

In the online surveys I focused mainly on establishing a ranking of priorities in terms of filtering searches, to find out what they consider more basic and what not, or more secondary. This will be important and will be clearly reflected in how the searches will be structured.

Even though, where I could gather more information was from the interviews. From them I got very valuable information to create a really interesting website for the user and not one to only use very occasionally. The most important point is to keep the web up to date with news and information from video games, mainly on issues of updates or bugs, offers, etc. The idea is not to create a news blog, but to show users which games have the most attention from their creators.

After analyzing the results I reached several conclusions that together with the market analysis explained above, I will comment on the following point.

Conclusions

Actually there is not a clear reference for a online video game recommender. There are just a bunch of different websites that seems to just show them mighty interesting video games, but not having in consideration users real likes or what they are more interested about.

At the same time navigation and usability is very poor in most of these websites. This is very important due to people tend to perceive attractive products as more usable. Marketing strategy and SEO is very poor on this websites too.

On the other hand, even though a video game recommender is an element every video game consumer will like to use from time to time, just with that service it isn’t enough to create an stable website. That said, creating a community which players can punctuate and comment which games they like most or no is a very interesting idea. Also creating reward system having in consideration users contributions making their profile stand out and establishing a ranking.

Information about which games have updates, next releases, etc. is another big point to consider, along with facilitating access to buy them.

Ideation Phase

Personas

After collecting all the information obtained from the surveys and interviews, I decided to create an affinity map, that is, a map with common thoughts according to the different answers given by each person. This led me to conclude that there will be 3 types of people mainly who will use this website. I have divided them into the following categories and then proceed to create their persona.

All of them are profiles that benefit from being able to filter searches, but you have to take into account that not everyone reaches with the same level of depth, so you have to establish a good hierarchy so that it is both accessible to all and interesting in your options .

  • The tireless player:
    This profile is characterized by being the most active. He is a person who plays daily or very frequently and likes to be very aware of the news in the world of video games.
  • The active, but scattered:
    He/She is a person who has been in the world of videogames for some time, but is not so fond of it. He/She likes to know in general what news there are, but doesn't investigate so much.
  • The casual:
    It is a type of player not so active who is more interested in a particular game and looks for information about it or search for similar games before buying it.

This is an example of one of the personas created.

Customer Journey Map

To begin to know how our website should be structured it is very useful to create a map with the path that a user would take in a given scenario and with a clear objective. Such as buying a new game. I decided to create 3, each with an action that could be performed by any of the people created in the previous step and divided into different phases according to the actions that are required.

This process is useful to determine key and pain points, where to pay more attention to detail and the creation of these, as well as new opportunities. For example, thanks to the map shown in the image, I could be able to detect how important it is not to make the search too long, as it will cause the user to get bored. Do not let him lose that emotion of looking for a new game to enjoy.

This is an example of one of the journeys created.

Design

After I organized all the research done and defined how the app was going to be, I started to think of potencial designs for the website. User Interface

The pallete is composed by a primary and secondary color, with a lighter tone for each one. Color palette is based on purples. But why choosing this color? Purple is associated with wisdom, dignity, independence, creativity, mystery, and magic. We believe that tattoos are a way to express ourselves (creativity) in the way we want, being clear or just with subtle touches (mystery) in the only way you want. This is the magic that resembles tattoo world.

Text is simple, I used Roboto typography since it matches perfectly the app style, being very easy to read and clear. I played with sizes and bold/regular, to create different sensation with the same typography.

Talking about corners, there are two types, completely round or slightely. It can be seen for example on buttons, which follow this guides.

Onboarding

The images on the intro screens are meant to represent a the main features of the app, so you’ll already know what you’ll find on the app. I focussed on using simple icons to make the experience easy and understandable. Each screen has a different message that clarifies the app’s intent.

From this you can log in or sign up, depending on if you have an account. To create an account you can select if you’re a tattoo artist and give more information about you, such as where you work at or your main styles.

Dashboard

Before continuing, I created a simple prototype to test by myself how intuitive the app was, did the changes necessary and after that, I conducted some usability tests with 6 people from my target audience. I asked the testers to do 3 different actions from the core features. During the test, I observed users actions and asked them to think aloud as much as possible, so I could take notes on everything they noticed, good and bad things.

Search

The idea behind this feature is that users can search to find exactly what they want regardless if it is a tattoo, an artist or and studio. Each tab will have each own filters to have more accurate results basing on user’s likes. This way we want to make sure, everyone has the opportunity to find their inspiration and the right tattoo for them.

Profile

As I said before there are different two kind of profiles depending on if you are or not an artist. Everyone will have a profile picture, be able to upload/like/save a photo as they want and more basic things, but on artist profile there will be another important feature. People who has done a tattoo by them, will be able to post a comment on their profile and punctuate artist. This way when people search for an artist, can see also what other people think about he/she.

Inbox

Why an inbox feature on app that is focused on searching tattoos? Because the idea is also to connect people with the same passion and there is another characteristic in which users can request a consultation for a tattoo and they will be able to put in contact with the artist they desire. They will be asked to leave a comment, style, size and place where they want to have the tattoo done. The artist will recieve this request and proceed to accept or decline it and anwer the user..

Final Thoughts

Along this project I learned to take the time needed for every individual part and indulge each one as they should be. This way is the best to get everything done correctly, so you won’t have to go back and forward to make sure everything is done as it should. It might sound obvious, but sometime the desire to continue and impatiente to see the final result may take the control and you can obviate things that shouldn’t or forget to consider important thing.

I loved doing this app and learning from everything I’ve done. I think it’s impressive to see how many people think differentely, but at the same time they want the same. Why I say this? Because while developing the app, I could talk with a lot of people and many of them gave different ideas of what they wanted, and I had to thought about a way of making a common point for them, since the main objective was to help on the tattoo community with my project.

I think I’ve managed to commit my objective, with an app where you can search and filter results of tattoos, studios and artist. Just in one app you can do several things and create your boards, so you can organize pics as you want. Also you can talk and request an appointment with an artist or an artist show his/her portfolio. This app is a way to simplify every tattoo lovers needs.

Hope you liked it!
